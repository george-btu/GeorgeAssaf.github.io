About Me

Hello, I'm George Assaf, a passionate Software Engineer and Researcher with a diverse background. In the early stages of my career, I focused on exploring the fascinating world of network protocol security. My research revolved around developing algorithms and data structures to enhance the security of network protocols, especially in the context of mobile ad hoc networks. As my career evolved, I developed a profound interest in Systems Biology modeling and simulation. My current research focuses on employing advanced approaches, particularly Coloured Petri nets, to model and simulate complex biological systems. Through this, I aim to contribute to the understanding of biological processes at a deeper level. In the realm of Systems Biology, I find Coloured Petri nets exceptionally promising for their applications in accurately modeling dynamic behaviors and complex interactions within biological systems. I am dedicated to exploring new ways to harness these Petri net-based techniques to study various biological phenomena, ranging from cellular processes to large-scale systems. My passion for developing innovative algorithms and data structures continues to drive my research endeavors, as I strive to create efficient and powerful tools for Systems Biology modeling and simulation. I believe that the synergy of software engineering and biological research holds great potential in pushing the boundaries of scientific understanding. If you share an interest in Systems Biology, Coloured Petri nets, or are curious about the intersections of software engineering and biology, I would love to connect and explore potential collaborative opportunities. Let's dive deeper into the fascinating world of modeling biological systems and making discoveries through computational methodologies! Technically speaking, I love C++, and all of my former research works have been primarily accomplished by implementing methods and algorithms using C++.
