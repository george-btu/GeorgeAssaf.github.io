Short Bio

I hold a PhD degree in Computer Science, specialized in Data Structures and Software Dependability. I work as Software Engineer and Researcher with a diverse background. In the early stages of my career, I focused on exploring the fascinating world of network protocol security. As my career evolved, I developed a profound interest in Systems Biology modelling and simulation. My current research focuses on employing advanced approaches, particularly coloured Petri nets, to model and simulate complex biological systems. Through this, I aim to contribute to the understanding of biological processes at a deeper level. In the realm of Systems Biology, I find coloured Petri nets exceptionally promising for their applications in accurately modeling dynamic behaviors and complex interactions within biological systems. I am dedicated to exploring new ways to harness Petri net-based techniques to study various biological phenomena, ranging from cellular processes to large-scale systems. My passion for developing innovative algorithms and data structures continues to drive my research endeavors, as I strive to create efficient and powerful tools for Systems Biology modeling and simulation. I believe that the synergy of software engineering and biological research holds great potential in pushing the boundaries of scientific understanding. Technically speaking, I love C++, and all of my former research works have been primarily accomplished by implementing methods and algorithms using C++.
